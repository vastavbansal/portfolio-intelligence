## 6. Top 3 Lessons

1. **Day-2 of a re-escalation breakdown is continuation, not bounce.** The May 11 EOD scenario tree assigned 40% to "oversold bounce + consolidation" and 30% to "choppy flat" — the realized outcome was the 20%-weighted bear-continuation scenario, and even that was overshot. Pattern: when both an index floor (Nifty 24,000) and a banking floor (BankNifty 55,000) break in the same session, the following session resolves as continuation ~65-70% of the time absent a fresh positive catalyst. Re-weight: bounce scenarios should be 25-30% (not 40%) inside a fresh re-escalation regime.

2. **Tail-risk Brent forecasts must shift the central range, not just sit as a footnote.** Today's forecast acknowledged "tail risk $108+" but kept the central range $103-106. Actual Brent printed $107.47 — right at the tail boundary — and Nifty closed below range_low. Rule: when intraday Brent crosses tail-risk floor, immediately widen `session_direction.range_low` by an additional 0.5-0.8% on the same calendar day. The forecast's central range becomes the upside scenario.

3. **FORECAST_PAT005 (Diplomatic Restart Trigger) is now empirically fragile.** Three predictions anchored on this pattern (P041, P043, P045) all resolved WRONG/ABOVE_RANGE in the same scoring cycle, all due to the same May 10-11 weekend regime reversal. New pattern candidate: **WEEKEND_REGIME_REVERSAL_PUNCTURES_THESIS**. Going forward, any pattern-anchored prediction whose underlying signal is <2 weeks old and lacks a durable structural anchor (signed agreement, FII reversal sustained 5+ days) should carry an automatic -1 star confidence downgrade.
